Euro Dumplings

Pierogi: A Polish dumpling made of unleavened dough, typically filled with potatoes, cheese, sauerkraut, or meat. They are often boiled and then pan-fried.


Gnocchi: An Italian dumpling made from potatoes, flour, and eggs. They are soft and often served with sauces like pesto or marinara.


Maultaschen: A German dumpling that resembles a large ravioli, filled with meat, spinach, and spices. They are usually served in broth or pan-fried.

Asian Dumplings

Mandu: Korean dumplings that can be filled with a variety of ingredients, including meat, tofu, and vegetables. They can be steamed, boiled, or fried.


Jiaozi: Chinese dumplings made with a thin dough wrapper filled with ground meat and vegetables. They can be boiled, steamed, or pan-fried.


Gyoza: A Japanese version of dumplings, similar to jiaozi but typically with a thinner skin and often pan-fried for a crispy texture.
